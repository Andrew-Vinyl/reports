Executive Summary

Accelerated SEO Performance & The Path Forward

Over the last three months, we've laid a formidable foundation for CBS's ongoing SEO strategy, and the results are hitting faster and harder than anticipated. By establishing strong technical performance and a rigorous content architecture, CBS now commands 12 keywords at Position 1 on Google search results—up from near-zero visibility just a few months ago.

What sets CBS apart right now is our proactive approach to the future of search. With AI Engine Optimization (AEO) practices firmly in place, including comprehensive schema markups on core service pages and AI summarization options across your vast 242-article library, the site isn't just ranking well on traditional search engines; it's actively formatted for AI models like ChatGPT and Perplexity to read, digest, and cite CBS as an authoritative source.

As we celebrate these immediate wins, we are simultaneously steering the ship toward the next strategic phases. Our over-arching roadmap involves strengthening specific niche service clusters (like Private Equity and Aerospace & Defense) and, critically, rolling out a robust lead generation and conversion tracking pipeline. This will allow us to seamlessly map these impressive ranking gains directly to attributable revenue.

Phase 1

Keyword Research Methodology & ICP Alignment

Status: Strong Progress
Our approach to keyword research isn't about chasing every possible search term—it's about building domain authority methodically. We began by identifying over 1,000 highly contextual keywords that map directly to your decision-makers' pain points. By picking battles we could win early in the "Lean Manufacturing" and "Continuous Improvement" spaces, we've successfully established a beachhead of topical authority that will make it easier to rank for more difficult terms later.

Strategic Highlights

  • Difficulty-Based Strategy: We successfully targeted moderate-difficulty keywords to build rapid topical authority. This initial momentum makes ranking for highly competitive terms easier moving forward.
  • Targeted ICPs: Our keywords are meticulously chosen to resonate with decision-makers at $250M+ enterprises in targeted sectors.
  • Immediate Successes: This strategy has directly resulted in our explosive growth, capturing page 1 for critical targeted terms like "lean manufacturing consulting" and "continuous improvement consulting services."

Roadmap: The Next Steps

Following our plan, the next phase involves translating this success into the more highly competitive spaces:

  • Private Equity & Aerospace/Defense: These are historically high-difficulty spaces. With our general authority now established, our strategy shifts toward attacking these highly specific, competitive clusters with targeted comparison content and tailored case studies.
  • Middle-Funnel Development: Expanding into mid-funnel queries that help prospects compare methodologies and specific industry challenges before committing to consulting engagements.
Third-Party Audit Perspective

If another agency were to run an automated tool against the site today, they might note that we don't yet rank for the highest-volume "Private Equity" head terms. This is expected. Our strategy intentionally built authority via achievable segments first. We are now strategically positioned to scale into those more difficult conversations with dedicated, highly specific content drops over the next few quarters.

Phase 2

On-Page SEO Across Site

Status: Solid Foundation
Search engines value depth, structure, and internal connectivity. We have modernized and expanded the five core service pages into cornerstone content hubs. They now host deep explanations, structured FAQs, and embedded case studies showing real business impact (like a $70M operational saving). The structure signals exactly what CBS does to both readers and crawlers.

Strategic Highlights

  • Service Page Deep Dives: Core pages (Lean, PE, OpEx, A&D, Continuous Improvement) have been fortified with expanded word counts, case studies, and FAQ sections.
  • Hub-and-Spoke Architecture: Your blog operates as the "spokes" that constantly feed relevance and authority back into the main service "hubs". We see this playing out perfectly in our 5S Methodology series.
  • Dual Optimization: Content titles strike the perfect balance—engaging enough for human click-through rates, yet optimized specifically for search engine parsing.

Roadmap: The Next Steps

As the primary infrastructure is locked in, we are turning our focus to supplementary content types:

  • Pillar Guides: We plan to develop comprehensive, 2,000+ word definitive guides representing the ultimate resource on specific operational strategies.
  • Comparison Content: Searching for "consulting vs in-house solutions" is a common decision stage. We plan to build dedicated pages to confidently answer these queries.
Third-Party Audit Perspective

A third-party SEO scan might suggest expanding the word count on certain older pages to meet universal benchmarks. We view content length as a means to an end, not a strict rule. As part of our content roadmap, we are selectively expanding pages where deeper industry terminology and additional case studies will genuinely improve the user experience rather than adding fluff just for word counts.

Phase 3

Technical SEO Basics

Status: Excellent
Under the hood, the CBS website is exceptionally healthy. We ensure the website respects the speed, security, and crawlability rules set by Google so that nothing artificially depresses our content's ability to rank. Based on industry-standard benchmarking, the site is passing with flying colors.

Strategic Highlights

  • Excellent Health Score: The site consistently runs at a 92-94% health score based on extensive 50+ point technical signal checks.
  • Core Web Vitals: Page load speed, visual stability, and interactivity all pass Google's rigorous performance thresholds comfortably.
  • Clean Migrations: Legacy pages (such as the old OpEx page) were properly retired using canonicalization, ensuring we don't dilute the SEO strength of the improved v2 versions.

Roadmap: The Next Steps

  • Routine Maintenance: Technical SEO requires ongoing monitoring. We continually adjust small elements (refreshing orphaned pages, tweaking minification) to keep the site in top shape as it scales.

Phase 6

AI Engine Optimization (AEO) Readiness

Status: Highly Advanced
As search habits evolve to rely more on generative AI (like ChatGPT, Claude, and Google's AI Overviews), we've ensured CBS is fully equipped to be the cited expert. We are deploying forward-thinking tactics here that very few B2B enterprise consulting firms are currently utilizing.

Strategic Highlights

  • Full Schema Deployment: Our live verification confirms all five core service pages feature robust "FAQ" and "Service" structural markup. This code explicitly dictates your organizational data to AI models.
  • AI Summarization Hooks: Your blog now utilizes one-click AI summarization buttons linking to ChatGPT, Perplexity, and others, facilitating user engagement while teaching language models that CBS is a go-to source.
  • Dedicated LLM Brief: A live `llms.txt` file is actively guiding AI crawlers through the CBS expertise ecosystem automatically.
Third-Party Audit Perspective

Most automated or traditional SEO audit tools are still catching up to the AI revolution. An old-school audit might flag schema elements or miss recent implementations entirely simply because their crawlers are outdated. If another agency questions our structured data, our answer is simple: we can provide live, verified code tests proving that our AI schema infrastructure is actively rendering and fully complete on all major pages.

Phase 7

Analytics & Conversion Tracking

Status: Upcoming Phase
With traffic and page-1 rankings sharply increasing, our next major priority shifts away from simply getting traffic and toward measuring its exact dollar value. We have Google Analytics (GA4) correctly monitoring the site visually, but the overarching strategic plan focuses on how we convert these visitors.

Roadmap: The Next Steps

This is arguably the most exciting upcoming phase of our engagement. We are actively finalizing a proposal to turn the CBS website into an easily-traceable lead generation machine. Once approved, the plan includes:

  • Lead Magnets: Implementing high-value downloadable resources (like assessments or whitepapers) to capture mid-funnel prospect information.
  • Smart Forms & CRM Integration: Capturing lead intent and sending it directly into HubSpot to start tailored sales workflows.
  • Attribution Modeling: Modifying our GA4 event tracking so we can point to an exact keyword and show exactly how many qualified leads it generated this quarter.
Third-Party Audit Perspective

A third-party audit would rightly point out that without sophisticated middle-funnel conversion tracking, we cannot calculate exact ROI for these new organic visits. We completely agree! That's exactly why building out the lead generation infrastructure and full-loop HubSpot tracking is our main prerogative going into this next phase of the strategy.
